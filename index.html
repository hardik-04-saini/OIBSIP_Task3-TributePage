Bhagat Singh

Revolutionary socialist and freedom fighter.

About the Legend

Bhagat Singh, born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, British India, was a revolutionary socialist and freedom fighter who played a significant role in the Indian independence movement.

Inspired by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and the non-cooperation movement led by Mahatma Gandhi, Bhagat Singh joined the struggle for independence at a young age. He became involved in revolutionary activities against British rule and was a prominent member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA).

Bhagat Singh is remembered for his acts of defiance against British colonialism, including the Lahore Conspiracy Case and the assembly bombing incident. His commitment to the cause of freedom and his fearlessness in the face of oppression made him a symbol of resistance and patriotism.

On March 23, 1931, Bhagat Singh was executed by the British colonial government at the age of 23, along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, for his involvement in the killing of British police officer John Saunders.

Bhagat Singh's legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians to fight against injustice and tyranny, and he remains a revered figure in the history of India's struggle for independence.
